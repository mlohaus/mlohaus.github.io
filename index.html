Hi,

my name is Michael and welcome to my website.

Currently, I am in the field of fairness in machine learning where we study the real-world impact of machine learning. In particular, I study fair decision-making problems, where we would like important decisions to be fair to groups of individuals. On the other hand, I also study Comparison-based learning, where we are not given representations of data points, but we know if a distance from item A to item B is smaller than the distance from item A to item C. My interests also lie in the fields of Explainability and Interpretability.

In October 2017, I started my PhD in the Theory of Machine Learning group (TML) supervised by Prof.Dr. Ulrike von Luxburg at Tuebingen university. I am a scholar in the International Max Planck Research School for Intelligent Systems (IMPRS-IS), a graduate school by both the universities and Max-Planck-Institutes in Tuebingen and Stuttgart.
